## 🔍 Understanding Vector Search

You might wonder how a computer understands the meaning behind your messages. Letheo uses a process called embeddings. This converts your words into a list of numbers. Similar words end up with similar numbers. When you ask a question, the software translates your intent into numbers and finds the closest match in your history. 

This process mimics human memory. It ignores exact word matches and focuses on the idea or intent behind the text. This allows your agent to answer questions even if you use different words than those recorded in the past.

## ❓ Troubleshooting

Most users find the setup process simple. If you encounter issues, follow these steps to fix common problems.

### The program fails to launch
Ensure your Windows system is up to date. Sometimes the computer blocks the program because it does not recognize the publisher. Click "More Info" on the blue warning window and select "Run anyway."

### The agent does not save memory
Check if you have enough space on your hard drive. The application needs a small amount of space to index your history. Also, confirm that you have granted the agent permission to talk to the memory engine during your agent setup.

### The search feels slow
If you have stored terabytes of data, the initial search might take a second longer. Letheo optimizes its index during idle times. Leave the application open for a few minutes to allow it to finish organizing your files.
